Internet Fraud and Scams:

Scammers defraud millions of dollars from people each year by using internet services and software. The tricks used by scammers can deceive people by sending money or giving out personal information. That’s why it’s important to protect yourself and know what you are up against.

  • Phising or Spoofing

    This scam involves the usage of fake emails, text messages, or fake duplicate websites that commit identity theft. These methods are used to steal personal information including credit card numbers, debit card pins, and more account information.

  • Malware

    Malware is a dangerous software, also commonly known as a virus, that is designed to disable or limit computers and systems.

  • Tracking

    A lot of websites nowadays might track your browsing activity. The reasons for this vary, and although in some cases, it’s designed just to make your browsing experience faster and more convenient, but not always. The data they keep of you can be used to determine browsing habits, preferences, information for other companies like advertisers. These can be a serious issue if you value your privacy.

  • Privacy

    Your browser activity is not as private as you may like to think. Depending on what browser, search engine, wifi you are on and more, you could be watched by someone else. Some websites are also not equipped with a secure connection, which is seen to the left of the url as a lock sign that will display if it is.
